# Online Shopping or Selling? Just be Careful Out There…

> Verbatim archived copy. Canonical machine record: `27331-online-shopping-or-selling-just-be-careful-out-there.json`.

<p style="text-align: justify;"><em>Ruses and tricks to separate the gullible from their hard-earned cash are many and cunning. Here are some tips to stay safe.</em></p>

<div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">

<strong>When it comes to online shopping, it’s not just customers who fall victim to scams. Businesses and sellers are frequent targets too.</strong>

<img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-27332" src="https://cfi.co/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/Online-1024x678.webp" alt="Online" width="900" height="596" />

According to <a href="https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20230801005682/en/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Business Wire</a>, payment fraud attempts on US businesses increased by 71 percent in 2023, highlighting the importance of vigilance.

Dennis Pederson, CEO of FastoPayments, has compiled a list of common payment scams that business owners should be aware of — and how to respond.
<h3>Baiting the Hook: Phishing Scams</h3>
Phishing scams trick people into sharing sensitive information, such as credit card details or passwords. Scammers typically send fake emails or texts linking to websites that mimic legitimate businesses. Once users click the link and enter their information, it’s stolen.

Businesses affected by phishing may suffer financial and data losses, which can erode customer trust and damage reputations.

<strong>How to Protect Yourself:</strong>
<ul>
 	<li>Educate employees on red flags like unexpected requests for personal data, spelling errors, suspicious senders, or emails with urgent tones.</li>
 	<li>Avoid clicking unfamiliar links or downloading suspicious attachments.</li>
 	<li>Implement advanced email filters, multi-factor authentication, and ensure software is always up-to-date.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Chargeback Fraud: The “Friendly” Scam</h3>
Chargeback fraud, or “friendly fraud,” occurs when a customer disputes a legitimate charge after receiving their order. They may falsely claim they didn’t receive the item or that the payment wasn’t authorised.

Unlike true fraud, these scammers appear trustworthy, but their actions cost businesses through losses and chargeback fees.

<strong>How to Protect Yourself:</strong>
<ul>
 	<li>Ensure merchant names and transaction details are clear on banking apps to avoid confusion.</li>
 	<li>Enable package tracking and delivery updates to confirm goods are received.</li>
 	<li>Use two-factor authentication for payments and verify large orders before shipping.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Return Fraud: Manipulating Policies</h3>
Return fraud involves customers exploiting return policies to obtain refunds illegitimately. They might return different items, claim products were defective, or use items briefly before returning them.

<strong>How to Protect Yourself:</strong>
<ul>
 	<li>Develop clear, non-negotiable return policies requiring items to be returned in original condition with tags intact.</li>
 	<li>Inspect returned goods thoroughly and monitor transactions for suspicious patterns.</li>
 	<li>Implement delivery tracking to confirm authenticity.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Merchant Fraud: Fake Businesses</h3>
Merchant fraud involves scammers posing as legitimate businesses to deceive customers. They may sell counterfeit or low-quality products or fail to deliver goods altogether.

This fraud can tarnish genuine companies’ reputations, cause financial losses, and result in higher processing fees for the industry.

<strong>How to Protect Yourself:</strong>
<ul>
 	<li>Ensure company names, logos, and transaction details appear on bank statements for transparency.</li>
 	<li>Secure your payment methods with multi-factor authentication and clear terms and conditions.</li>
 	<li>Regularly update software and stay informed on fraud prevention techniques.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Wire Transfer Fraud: Tricking the Trusted</h3>
Wire transfer fraud involves impersonation of trusted individuals or organisations to deceive businesses into transferring money. Scammers create fake invoices or urgent requests, preying on emotions and tight deadlines.

<strong>How to Protect Yourself:</strong>
<ul>
 	<li>Never send money unexpectedly. Ensure transactions are approved by multiple parties.</li>
 	<li>Improve cybersecurity protocols, use strong passwords, and guard sensitive banking details.</li>
 	<li>Educate employees to double-check invoice authenticity and seek second opinions when in doubt.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<h3 style="text-align: justify;">FAQs About Online Shopping and Selling Safety</h3>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<h4>What are the most common online scams targeting sellers?</h4>
<div>

Common scams include phishing (stealing sensitive information), chargeback fraud (disputing legitimate purchases), return fraud (manipulating refund policies), and wire transfer fraud (sending fake invoices for payments).

</div>
</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<h4>How can businesses prevent phishing scams?</h4>
<div>

Businesses can educate employees on phishing red flags, use advanced email filtering tools, enable multi-factor authentication, and ensure digital systems are updated regularly.

</div>
</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<h4>What is chargeback fraud, and how can sellers combat it?</h4>
<div>

Chargeback fraud occurs when customers dispute legitimate charges. Sellers can combat it by clarifying merchant names on bank statements, providing package tracking, and verifying large orders before shipping.

</div>
</div>
<div>
<h4 style="text-align: justify;">What should businesses do if they suspect wire transfer fraud?</h4>
<div>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Businesses should contact their bank immediately to stop the transaction. Preventative measures include improving cybersecurity, using strong passwords, and ensuring multiple approvals for transactions.</p>
