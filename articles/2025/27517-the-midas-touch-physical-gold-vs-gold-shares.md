# The Midas Touch: Physical Gold vs Gold Shares

> Verbatim archived copy. Canonical machine record: `27517-the-midas-touch-physical-gold-vs-gold-shares.json`.

<p style="text-align: justify;"></p>

<h2 style="text-align: justify;">Gold: A Hedge Against Uncertainty</h2>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Gold has long been considered a safe haven in times of economic instability. As <a href="https://www.forbes.com/sites/qai/2024/01/05/is-gold-a-good-investment-in-2024/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Forbes</a> reports, gold prices tend to rise during geopolitical tensions, making it a popular hedge against inflation and currency depreciation.</p>
<img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-27518" src="https://cfi.co/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/Gold-1024x682.jpg" alt="Gold" width="900" height="599" />
<p style="text-align: justify;">But when it comes to investing, there’s a fundamental decision to be made: should you hold <strong>physical gold</strong> (bars, coins, or jewellery), or invest in <strong>gold-related assets</strong> such as stocks, ETFs, and mutual funds? Each option comes with its own benefits and risks.</p>

<h2 style="text-align: justify;">Investing in Physical Gold</h2>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Physical gold is <strong>tangible, universally recognised, and not reliant on financial markets</strong>. Investors often see it as a <strong>wealth preservation tool</strong>, especially in times of economic crisis.</p>

<h3 style="text-align: justify;">Advantages of Physical Gold</h3>
<ul style="text-align: justify;">
 	<li><strong>Store of Value:</strong> Gold has retained its purchasing power for centuries.</li>
 	<li><strong>Inflation Hedge:</strong> Gold often rises in value when paper currencies weaken.</li>
 	<li><strong>Global Recognition:</strong> Gold is easily tradable worldwide.</li>
</ul>
<h3 style="text-align: justify;">Challenges of Physical Gold</h3>
<ul style="text-align: justify;">
 	<li><strong>Storage &amp; Security:</strong> Storing gold securely incurs extra costs.</li>
 	<li><strong>Liquidity Concerns:</strong> Selling gold isn’t as seamless as trading stocks.</li>
 	<li><strong>Premiums &amp; Fees:</strong> Dealer markups, storage fees, and insurance costs can add up.</li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify;">For those looking to buy physical gold, popular options include:</p>

<ul style="text-align: justify;">
 	<li><a href="https://www.usmint.gov/bullion-programs/american-eagle" target="_blank" rel="noopener">American Eagle Gold Coins</a></li>
 	<li><a href="https://www.mint.ca/store/mint/learn/Gold-products-1600004" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Canadian Maple Leaf</a></li>
 	<li><a href="https://www.randrefinery.com/krugerrand/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">South African Krugerrand</a></li>
</ul>
<h2 style="text-align: justify;">Gold Stocks &amp; ETFs</h2>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Instead of holding physical gold, investors can gain exposure through <strong>gold stocks, ETFs, and mutual funds</strong>. These options allow for easier trading and lower upfront costs.</p>

<h3 style="text-align: justify;">Gold Mining Stocks</h3>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Investing in mining companies such as <a href="https://www.barrick.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Barrick Gold</a> and <a href="https://www.newmont.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Newmont Corporation</a> offers <strong>leverage on gold prices</strong>—when gold prices rise, mining profits often increase exponentially.</p>

<h3 style="text-align: justify;">Gold ETFs</h3>
<p style="text-align: justify;">ETFs such as <a href="https://www.spdrgoldshares.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">SPDR Gold Shares (GLD)</a> track gold prices without requiring physical ownership, offering liquidity and diversification.</p>

<h3 style="text-align: justify;">Gold Mutual Funds</h3>
<p style="text-align: justify;">These funds, managed by professionals, invest in a mix of mining companies and gold-related assets.</p>

<h2 style="text-align: justify;">Final Thoughts: Which Option is Right for You?</h2>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Gold can be a valuable addition to an investment portfolio, but <strong>choosing between physical gold and gold stocks depends on your goals</strong>.</p>

<ul style="text-align: justify;">
 	<li>If you’re looking for <strong>wealth preservation and a hedge against crisis</strong>, physical gold might be the better option.</li>
 	<li>If you prioritise <strong>liquidity and potential growth</strong>, gold stocks and ETFs offer an easier, more flexible alternative.</li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Regardless of your choice, experts recommend <strong>allocating 5-10% of your portfolio</strong> to gold for diversification.</p>
