# Kim Jenkins: Building Vistra’s Next Chapter Around Digital Scale and Client Choice

> Verbatim archived copy. Canonical machine record: `28549-kim-jenkins-building-vistras-next-chapter-around-digital-scale-and-client-choice.json`.

[caption id="attachment_28551" align="alignright" width="300"]<img class="size-medium wp-image-28551" src="https://cfi.co/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Vistra-CEO-300x297.jpg" alt="CEO Kim Jenkins" width="300" height="297" /> <strong>CEO:</strong> Kim Jenkins[/caption]
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;"><strong>For Kim Jenkins, Group CEO of Vistra, the next phase of the company’s growth is defined by execution. The strategic direction has already been set: to become a digitally powered professional services leader. Her mandate is to ensure that vision is delivered consistently, market by market, client by client, and colleague by colleague.</strong></p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">Over the next 12 to 18 months, Jenkins is focused on three priorities. The first is building a single digital backbone so that clients experience one Vistra, regardless of geography. The second is simplifying the organisation around empowered market leaders who own the end-to-end client relationship. The third is ensuring that every colleague becomes AI-literate, equipped not only to adapt to technological change but to compete more effectively in a transforming industry.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">The appointment of Damian Leach as Chief AI and Digital Officer, together with the creation of a Digital Subcommittee of the Board, underlines the strategic importance of this agenda. For Vistra, digital transformation is not a side project. It is a board-level priority and a core driver of future competitiveness.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">Jenkins describes “the next level” as a proposition that must work simultaneously for clients, colleagues, and shareholders. For clients, it means a seamless, anywhere-anytime experience in which dealing with Vistra in the UK, Singapore, Hong Kong, or elsewhere feels like working with one company. They gain access to a digital portal supported by deep human expertise and can choose how far along the digital journey they wish to go.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">For colleagues, the next level means work that feels organised, professional, and empowering, rather than reactive or fragmented. Vistra is seeking to recover between 30 and 50 percent of time currently spent on non-value work, while equipping people with AI tools that enhance their roles rather than threaten them. For shareholders, the model points to sustainable growth through client retention, geographic expansion, and operational leverage as the digital backbone reaches scale.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">Client centricity sits at the heart of the strategy. Jenkins describes it as Vistra’s primary strategic DNA, with product innovation and operational excellence existing to support the client experience. Yet she is clear that seamless service begins with people, not technology.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">One of her key changes has been to simplify Vistra’s structure around the market leader. This is where clients meet the organisation, and where the organisation meets its clients. The entire company is now organised to support those leaders in delivering better outcomes.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">Digital capability then amplifies that human structure. Through Vistra Digital, clients can view their entity estate, upload documents, track service requests, and communicate with Vistra across markets. For some clients, the preferred route will be extensive digital self-service. Others will continue to favour direct human interaction. Vistra’s approach is to offer choice rather than impose a single model.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">That flexibility matters in a sector where local rules, tax systems, documentation requirements, and regulatory obligations vary widely. A client may need to access a document while travelling across time zones, or obtain reassurance on a complex compliance matter. Digital tools improve access and transparency, but professional expertise remains essential. Jenkins’ view is clear: a broken structure cannot be fixed by digitisation alone. The human operating model must be simplified first, then supported by technology.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">The same principle shapes her approach to transformation leadership. Large organisations move faster when accountability is clear. Jenkins has therefore placed emphasis on simplification, single ownership, and open two-way communication. Transformation, in her view, only works when people feel heard, not merely informed. Colleagues must be able to ask questions, challenge decisions, and share what is working on the ground. That feedback loop helps the business course-correct in real time and preserves intelligence closest to the client.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">Implementation is another priority. Large-scale programmes often fail not because the ambition is wrong, but because insufficient attention is paid to the “how”. Vistra is therefore rolling out change market by market, supported by on-ground teams that stay to help embed new ways of working.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">Leaders are also expected to move first. Vistra’s first 250 AI users were members of the leadership team, ensuring they understood the tools well enough to support their teams. More than 1,100 colleagues have since volunteered to become AI champions, creating a grassroots movement around experimentation and adoption.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">AI is already reshaping Vistra’s operating model through Vistra Digital. The platform brings together digital capability, professional expertise, and client choice. Geni, the company’s AI assistant, helps speed up service requests and surface answers quickly. Clients may choose self-service or work directly with specialists, with expert support remaining within reach.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">The platform also gives clients proactive visibility over compliance deadlines, regulatory changes, and operational matters that do not respect business hours or time zones. A unified portfolio view enables clients to manage entities serviced by Vistra, or bring in a broader estate, through one workflow hub. The system is designed to scale as clients expand into new markets and require additional services.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">Behind the scenes, AI is helping advisers research, prepare, scan regulatory environments, and monitor changing legislation more quickly. It is also accelerating client onboarding, entity management, compliance workflows, and payroll operations.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">Yet Jenkins draws a firm line around human judgement. AI can help an adviser prepare for a client conversation, but it cannot replace the assurance of a trusted professional relationship. Clients do not simply want an action completed. They want confidence that they are compliant, in good standing, and supported by people who understand the relevant regulations.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">This is particularly important in high-stakes compliance and governance decisions, where the cost of error can be significant. Vistra’s position is that technology without expertise creates risk, while expertise without technology creates inefficiency. Its aim is to combine both: modern digital capability backed by experienced professionals.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">Culture is central to sustaining that balance. Vistra has grown through 18 acquisitions over 15 years, creating scale but also the risk of losing entrepreneurial energy. Jenkins is seeking to protect speed and accountability by empowering market leaders with full profit-and-loss responsibility, including ownership of sales, account management, and delivery. This preserves local accountability while operating within a global framework.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">Governance is being strengthened without creating unnecessary bureaucracy. The Digital Subcommittee of the Board and the Chief AI and Digital Officer role provide strategic oversight, while execution remains with empowered teams in the markets. Innovation is encouraged from the bottom up as well as directed from the top, with the AI champions programme drawing ideas from across Vistra’s 9,000 colleagues.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">Change management is deliberately phased. By moving market by market over weeks and months, supported by dedicated teams, Vistra aims to respect local context and avoid change fatigue. The goal is to become more unified, efficient, and client-focused while remaining a place where people feel agency, move quickly, and take pride in the organisation.</p>
<p class="isSelectedEnd" style="text-align: justify;">For Jenkins, Vistra’s ambition is not simply to digitise corporate and fund services. It is to redefine how clients experience a global professional services provider. The company’s philosophy is straightforward: clients choose how they work, and Vistra’s experts handle the rest.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">That combination of digital scale, human expertise, and client choice is shaping the next chapter of Vistra’s growth. If delivered well, it will allow the organisation to simplify complexity for clients, empower colleagues with better tools, and build a more agile platform for long-term global expansion.</p>
